## What to Ask (Outreach Script)

Use this checklist in your first email or form submission. Customize the bracketed fields.

### Opening (one paragraph)
> We are conducting mineral title / chain-of-title research in [County] County and need to understand how your office provides **bulk Official Public Records (OPR) index data** (not individual copy orders). We are interested in deeds, mineral deeds, oil & gas leases, assignments, releases, royalty deeds, pooling/designation documents, and related OPR instruments. Could you please answer the questions below or point us to the correct department contact?

### Specific questions

1. **Bulk index export**
   - Do you sell or provide a **machine-readable OPR index export** (CSV, pipe-delimited, XML, or database dump)?
   - If yes: what **fields** are included (instrument number, recorded date, document type, grantor, grantee, legal description, book/page, consideration, etc.)?
   - Can we filter by **document type** (e.g., mineral deed, O&G lease, assignment, release, royalty deed)?

2. **Images**
   - Are **document images** available in bulk (TIFF, PDF, or other)? Separate from index or bundled?
   - What **resolution/format** and naming convention do you use?

3. **FTP / recurring delivery**
   - Do you offer **FTP, SFTP, or recurring subscription** delivery?
   - If yes: setup process, credentials, update frequency (daily/weekly/monthly), and whether delivery is incremental or full snapshot.

4. **Coverage & date ranges**
   - What is the **earliest and latest** recorded date available electronically?
   - Are there **gaps** (missing images, index-only periods, redactions under HB 4350 or similar)?
   - Approximately how many OPR documents are recorded per year?

5. **Mineral / O&G instrument types**
   - Which **document type codes** in your system correspond to mineral deeds, O&G leases, assignments, releases, and pooling/designation instruments?
   - Are mineral instruments reliably indexed under both grantor and grantee names?

6. **Fees & payment**
   - Fee schedule for bulk index, bulk images, FTP subscription, and one-time setup?
   - Minimum order size, prepayment requirements, and accepted payment methods?
   - Typical **turnaround time** from payment to delivery?

7. **PIA alternative**
   - If no commercial bulk program exists: can a **Texas Public Information Act (Gov. Code Ch. 552)** request be used for an existing **index export or database extract**?
   - Who is your **public information officer** for County Clerk OPR records, and is there a preferred written request format?

8. **Technical / legal**
   - Are there **restrictions** on redistribution or commercial use of bulk data?
   - Do you provide **data dictionary / file layout** documentation?
   - Is there a **sample file** we can review before purchase?

9. **Online search API**
   - Is your public search portal ([vendor name if known]) available for **bulk/automated access** under a separate agreement?

| Others | Ask clerk directly whether PIA applies to existing index/database exports (cannot require creation of new reports in some cases) |

**PIA tips:** Request *existing* electronic records (database exports, index files already maintained). Be specific about format, date range, and document types. Expect labor/copy charges for large requests; ask for cost estimate before production.

## Document type codes to mention (mineral research)

When asking about filtering, reference these common OPR instrument categories (exact codes vary by county):

- Mineral deed / mineral conveyance
- Oil and gas lease (OGL)
- Assignment of O&G lease
- Partial assignment / release
- Royalty deed / royalty interest conveyance
- Pooling agreement / designation (if recorded in OPR)
- Memorandum of lease
- Overriding royalty assignment
- Unit declaration / declaration of pooled unit
